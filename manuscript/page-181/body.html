Nevertheless he felt a little prick of ambition, a sneaking desire to have another battle with that ptarmigan hen—only the hawk had carried her away.  May be there were other ptarmigan hens.  He would go and see.

He came down a shelving bank to the stream.  He had never seen water before.  The footing looked good.  There were no inequalities of surface.  He stepped boldly out on it; and went down, crying with fear, into the embrace of the unknown.  It was cold, and he gasped, breathing quickly.  The water rushed into his lungs instead of the air that had always accompanied his act of breathing.  The suffocation he experienced was like the pang of death.  To him it signified death.  He had no conscious knowledge of death, but like every animal of the Wild, he possessed the instinct of death.
