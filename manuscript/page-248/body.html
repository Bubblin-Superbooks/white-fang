She had not moved.  He whined pleadingly, and scurried playfully in and out of the underbrush.  He ran back to her, licked her face, and ran on again.  And still she did not move.  He stopped and regarded her, all of an intentness and eagerness, physically expressed, that slowly faded out of him as she turned her head and gazed back at the camp.

There was something calling to him out there in the open.  His mother heard it too.  But she heard also that other and louder call, the call of the fire and of man—the call which has been given alone of all animals to the wolf to answer, to the wolf and the wild-dog, who are brothers.
