He cringed involuntarily under the expected blow.  It did not fall.  He stole a glance upward.  Grey Beaver was breaking the lump of tallow in half!  Grey Beaver was offering him one piece of the tallow!  Very gently and somewhat suspiciously, he first smelled the tallow and then proceeded to eat it.  Grey Beaver ordered meat to be brought to him, and guarded him from the other dogs while he ate.  After that, grateful and content, White Fang lay at Grey Beaver’s feet, gazing at the fire that warmed him, blinking and dozing, secure in the knowledge that the morrow would find him, not wandering forlorn through bleak forest-stretches, but in the camp of the man-animals, with the gods to whom he had given himself and upon whom he was now dependent.
