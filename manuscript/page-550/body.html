“Go to it,” he said to White Fang.

But White Fang could not believe.  He looked at the master, and he looked at the dogs.  Then he looked back eagerly and questioningly at the master.

The master nodded his head.  “Go to them, old fellow.  Eat them up.”

White Fang no longer hesitated.  He turned and leaped silently among his enemies.  All three faced him.  There was a great snarling and growling, a clashing of teeth and a flurry of bodies.  The dust of the road arose in a cloud and screened the battle.  But at the end of several minutes two dogs were struggling in the dirt and the third was in full flight.  He leaped a ditch, went through a rail fence, and fled across a field.
